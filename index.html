Types of Hunter Jumper Shows

Hunter jumper shows range from informal schooling shows with relaxed rules and low entry fees through the most prestigious Premier-rated USEF competitions with significant prize money and national finals qualifying implications. Schooling shows are ideal for young horses gaining experience, developing riders building confidence, and any horse-rider combination testing a new level or division before committing to rated competition. USEF-rated shows — National (A), Regional (B), and Local (C) levels — provide the competitive experience, qualifying points, and championship opportunities that build toward national goals.

Planning Your Show Season

A well-planned show season balances competitive development with the physical and mental wellbeing of both horse and rider. Consider which shows align with your qualification goals (Pony Finals, Junior Hunter Finals, NAL Finals, national equitation finals), which circuits provide the best combination of competition level and logistics for your program, and how many shows per month is appropriate for your horse's age, fitness, and competitive stage.
